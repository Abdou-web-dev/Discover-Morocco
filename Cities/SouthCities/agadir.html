Agadir

أݣادير

With a busy port and beach resort sprawling beneath its kasbah, Agadir was completely rebuilt following a devastating earthquake in 1960. It is now the country’s premier destination for sun, sand, pubs and pizza.

Laid out as a large grid of downtown streets, surrounded by spacious residential suburbs, Agadir’s concrete-covered inland quarters are sterile. However, the city hits its stride on the beachfront promenade, where Moroccan street life comes with a refreshing sense of space. Arching south of the shiny white marina, the sandy beach offers clean water and 300 sunny days a year.

Agadir caters mainly to package-tour holidaymakers and will appeal less to independent travellers. Families will enjoy relaxing on the beach and wandering around the handful of sights. If you do not have children in tow, however, we recommend heading elsewhere to make the most of a visit to Morocco.

Agadir's corniche, an idyllic place for a wonderful walk

The Corniche of Agadir, a place to walk in peace Between the Ibn Zidoun Garden, the white beach, or the Medina Polizzi, there are many things to visit in Agadir. But when you just want to take a stroll, there is nothing better than the Corniche de la Ville.

One of the nicest places in the city of Agadir is undoubtedly its ledge. Expertly appointed, well maintained and perfectly lit after dark, it is ideal for a stroll. People come here to play sports, admire the sea and / or have a drink in one of the cafes along the promenade. The view of the Casbah of Agadir - and of the inscription “Dieu, la Patrie, le Roi” on the mountain - is also one of the highlights of the corniche.
