Ait Ben Haddou

آيت بن حدو

Ait Ben Haddou is located about thirty kilometers from Ouarzazate in Morocco. This is called a ksar. A ksar is a fortified village, of Berber architecture, found in different regions of North Africa. In Morocco, they are quite present in southern Morocco. We also find ksar in Algeria or Tunisia. A ksar is often built on the buttress of a mountain and near an oasis. They are therefore similar to fortified villages that contain one or more granaries as well as dwellings. The word ksar comes from the Arabic qasr which means castle which itself comes from the Latin word castrum meaning stronghold.

Aït Ben Haddou is one of the most famous ksar in Morocco because it is impressive, rather well preserved and perfectly representative of what a ksar is. It is also famous for having been the location of several films, including the famous Gladiator.
