Figuig

Figuig is an oasis located in the southern eastern tip of Morocco about 400 km south of the Mediterranean and 7 km from the Algerian town of Beni Ounif. It is surrounded on three sides by Algeria; but the border between the two countries is now closed, which has created the isolation and isolation of the city,

which has experienced an intense migratory movement, which is explained by the sudden reduction in its economic resources. This, in part, caused a significant deterioration of its heritage, hence the urgency of its classification.

The oasis society of Figuig has developed over time a specific earthen architecture materially translating the structures of its organization and social, cultural and religious practices, which constitute an intangible heritage of great importance.

The oasis of Figuig is presented as a coherent, material and cultural whole, where there is a complementarity between the architecture and the spatial organization of the ksour,

the palm grove and its irrigation system and all social and cultural practices. This configuration is accentuated by its landlocked situation, generated by the closure of the Algerian border.

"The people here are linked to their land, their veins are irrigated by their roots"

Thus, Figuig is an oasis which has preserved from its history exceptional vestiges which today represent material heritage riches; architectural and archaeological important: great walls, ramparts, watchtowers, mosques, mausoleums, irrigation canals

Ksar of El-Hammam-el-Foukani

It contains the ancient Zaouïa of Sidi Bou Amama (1840 – 1908), native of the ksar, it was an emblematic figure of the uprising of the populations of the south of Oran, in Algeria, against the French occupation. To the south of the ksar behind a small door, a staircase, slippery attention, goes down to the hot spring at 33° which gives its name to the ksar.

Ksar Zenaga

A path bypasses the ksar el Oudaghir, from the west, and leads to hot and salty springs and a palm grove to reach the ksar of Zenaga, the farthest, but the largest ksour of the Figuig oasis.

Palm grove of Figuig

Rich of about 150 000 palm trees, the Figuig oasis is among the most beautiful in Morocco. Emerging from about twenty springs, the water is conveyed by gullies, seguias, or by underground channels, khettaras.

The terrace

400 m from the administrative centre of the Zenaga ksar. One discovers there a remarkable sight on the palm grove, the ksar of Zenaga and, further south, the pass of the same name. On the right, we discover the Grouz massif as well as the Jewish pass and finally, on the left, the jbel Taghla and the jbel Sidi Youssef.

Valley of the wadi Zousfana

The wadi Zousfana at the foot of the arid rocks of the jbel Sidi Youssef feeds a valley encased with abundant palm trees. 2 tracks reach the valley, passing north and south of the palm groves of El Hammam and El Maïz and meet on the heights of the cliff which dominates the valley of the wadi.
