Tinghir

Want to discover Tinghir? There are a thousand and one ways to experience it. Tinghir is a charming town known for its magnificent palm grove and its famous Todra gorges, it is built in floors around a promontory dominated by the ruins of an ancient Glaoui residence...

Tinghir is a town in central-eastern Morocco, in the region of Souss-Massa-Drâa, located in the Todgha valley, at the exit of the High Atlas and facing the jbel Saghro, then emerges into the great plain connecting the region of Ouarzazate to the Tafilalet to meander about twenty kilometers to Wadi Ferkla.

It is a privileged place for climbers for their walls that can reach 300 meters. These walls are very well equipped but many are still virgin.

Its name comes from the Tamazight tinghir, the place (tin) of the mountain (ighir, shoulder or ridge). It is populated by 40,000 inhabitants.

The palm grove, 30 kilometers long and filled with small ksouret kasbahs, stretches from one side of the city to the other. Walks are offered there to tourists, who discover a whole community organization, both in terms of work in the fields and the distribution of water.

15 km from the center of Tinghir, the Todra gorges, high limestone cliffs carved out by the Todra wadi for millennia, impress with their spectacular sheer drop plunging down to the river bed. These are impressive gorges in the Todra Valley that are the pride of Tinghir.

These are very popular with foreign tourists, traveling in the South East, but also by nationals.

Oasis of Tinerhir

Visible from the road that leads to the Todra Gorge, just outside the town of Tinghir, in the foothills of the Atlas, it is one of the most beautiful landscapes you can find in the Moroccan desert. It can be walked inland, looking at the many canals used by farmers to irrigate their crops (mostly corn), but the panoramic view offered from the road is sufficient.

Ikelane Mosque

The origin of the mosque is believed to be medieval, although its decoration and plaster arcades appear to be from the 19th century.

Amtoudi

Amtoudi is the village located at the outlet of the palm grove of the oasis of Oued Boulgour, called "Id-Aïssa" on the Michelin map. This village is a place to stay for a discovery of a magnificent region: Amtoudi is at the outlet of a spectacular canyon and, in the surroundings, no less than two granaries (agadir) constitute particularly aesthetic fortresses .

Aït-Boujane

Aït-Boujane is the first village that we meet after Tineghir going up the Todgha valley. From the road that leads to the famous Gorges, there is a panoramic view of this village which overlooks a lush palm grove. Like any old village, it has still preserved its mud houses dominated by the minaret of the recent mosque. Exceptional vision of a remarkable heritage in a setting of beautiful mountains.
Near the car park of this magnificent promontory, so-called guides invite to camel rides or to a pedestrian discovery of the palm grove.

Ighir

From a tourist point of view we mention the valley of Aït-Bougmez and that of Aït Boulli, but nobody says anything about the valley which is located between Tamernoute and Agouti and which makes us discover, almost always overhanging, the unique road which, from Azilal, leads to Tabant.

Oasis del Todra

Before reaching and tourist impressive Todra Gorges, we find the oasis surrounding this important river in the region. A place that usually people don't know and we 100% recommend spending a morning. Inside the oasis, alongside date palms, traditional agricultural crops are the most fertile lands in the region .
