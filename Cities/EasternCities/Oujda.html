Oujda

Want to discover Oujda? There are a thousand and one ways to experience it. The city of Oujda is one of the cities of Morocco where traditions are the most rooted because of its history and its conservative population. ...

There are remains of human activity from primitive times in the caves around Oujda: flint, points, etc. Specimens of Stone Age industry were collected around Oujda before the Romans arrived.

Oujda is a border town of Morocco, located in the extreme northeast of the country, on the edge of the Rif Oriental region, its proximity to the port of Nador and with Algeria at 5 km allows it to play the role crossroads between Morocco and Algeria, it has more than a million inhabitants.

60 km from Saïdia, inland, Oujda reveals the richness of its long history. Heritage is in the spotlight here. There is the medina and its monumental gates; there is the Sebti palace, a center of cultural life; finally, further on, the Lalla Aicha park, a haven of freshness in the very heart of the city.

The medina of Oujda with its Arab-Muslim character reached an area of ​​twenty-five hectares, sheltering several monuments of undeniable historical value, was surrounded by a belt of gardens planted with olive trees, a belt of a thousand meters thick in some places.

Each garden was enclosed by an adobe wall, 1.80 to 2 meters high, pierced with numerous holes. These gardens therefore constituted an appreciable defense system. Not so quickly, there is not only the medina to see, you will also see not far from the medina, the Lalla Aïcha Park extends over an area of ​​approximately 20 hectares.

Created in 1935, it is a place of relaxation for the inhabitants of Oujda. It offers a beautiful leisure area with its swimming pools, sports grounds, tennis and horse riding clubs as well as children's play equipment.

Lalla Meriem Park, designed for relaxation and housing a museum. This park is also the seat of the Tourist Office of the Oriental.

One can also a place to learn Gharnati music there. Its privileged geographical position of Oujda constitutes an asset for its prosperity and its progress, the city has a commercial and tertiary vocation, its economy is built mainly on the smuggling of goods from Algeria.

The Medina

Oujda's medina is a great slice of tradition and modernity. It isn't large and appeals to local tastes, so don't expect to wade through touts selling carpets, lanterns and cushions. Most shops sell Moroccan clothing, jewellery and housewares. The area near Bab El Wahab, the eastern gate, is chock-full of food stalls selling seasonal produce (Oujda olives are well regarded).

Lalla Aicha Park

Designed in the 1930s by René Maître, the city's chief architect, this beautiful park lined with magnificent trees and bright flowers is worth a stroll. There's a swimming pool (summer only), a cafe, tennis and horse riding.

Grande Mosquée

Towering over Oujda's small medina, the oldest mosque in the city, known to followers as Lkebir Jamaa, was built when Merinid ruler Sultan Abou Youssef rebuilt Oujda in the 13th century. Unfortunately, it's difficult to see the minaret from the narrow medina streets.

Bank Al Maghrib

The blockish central bank just south of Place 16 Août was built during the French protectorate in the 1920s. It's constructed in the Franco-Moorish style.

Clock Tower

Oujda's most recognisable landmark is the 1920s art-deco clock tower that overlooks Place 16 Août. The mosque next to it is arguably more impressive, however.

Tafoughalt

Tafoughalt or Taforalt, in Morocco, is a village located in the Beni Snassen mountains. It brings together several businesses where people living in the mountains generally come to get their supplies. With its waterfalls and its medlar plantations, it is also a tourist area under development.
